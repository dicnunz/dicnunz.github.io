Acceptance

What makes a record usable

  • Route openedThe reviewer names which public URLs were opened and can recover the route without private context.
  • One-sentence readThe reviewer states what CIW is in one sentence without clone, official, affiliation, or employment language.
  • Claim boundaryThe reviewer explicitly names blocked claims: no target-side outcome, no legal company, no customers, no affiliation.
  • Proof mechanismThe reviewer cites the hash-chain, graph, tests, route audit, or source repo as the proof mechanism.
  • Remaining confusionThe reviewer names at least one remaining question or explicitly says the route is clear.
  • Classification chosenThe record chooses usable_non_target_feedback, ambiguous_feedback, or target_owned_signal before next action.

Hard stops

What stays blocked

  • No outbound before 2026-05-26 unless a verified real reply or explicit fresh override changes the route.
  • External reviewer feedback is not OpenAI-side outcome evidence unless a separate target-company-owned source is verified.
  • No OpenAI affiliation, employment, endorsement, customer, legal, payment, or company-formation claim.
  • No private data, email address, raw message text, local path, credential, account data, or customer material in public records.
  • A blank intake record cannot raise the score; only classified public-safe feedback can repair the external-review gap.
